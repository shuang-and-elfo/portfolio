Designing Performant Ad Formats that Feel Native on Snapchat

Role

Design Lead

Company

Snap Inc.

Timeline

2021–2024

Scope

Full-funnel ad formats

Advertising design is often treated as a necessary evil—something to maximize for clicks while minimizing user complaints. I've always believed it can be more than that. The best ads don't feel like interruptions. They feel like they belong.

My time on Snap's ads team was spent pursuing that belief: designing formats that respect how people actually use Snapchat, deliver real value to advertisers, and protect the trust that makes our platform special. The best-performing ads didn't win by shouting louder. They won by feeling like part of the experience—same language, same rhythms, same expectations as organic content.

Designing for How People Actually Behave

Every design decision started with the same question: how do people naturally use Snapchat, and how can ads fit into that behavior rather than fight against it?

Setting interaction guardrails

Snapchat users tap quickly. They're skipping through Stories at a pace that makes accidental clicks almost inevitable. We introduced a delay before tap targets become active, and reserved screen edges for navigation. These guardrails dramatically improved click quality—ensuring that when someone taps, they meant to. At the system level, this work shifted success metrics away from raw CTR toward higher-quality engagement and more reliable downstream signals.

CTA progressive disclosure

CTAs evolve over time—subtle at first, then more prominent after 3+ seconds when we know someone is actually watching. The ad card appears after a short delay, then expands and becomes more prominent later, aligning with moments when users are more likely to engage intentionally.

We tested several variants of the delay (500ms, 750ms, 1000ms) and expansion (2.5s, 3s, 4s) and launched the most performant one (1s, 3s). Accidental clicks were reduced by 47%, while high-quality clicks increased by 33%.

Multi-segment story ads

People post multiple Snaps in a Story. It's a natural behavior—so why should ads be limited to one frame? I designed story ads that unfold across multiple segments, giving advertisers room to tell a story while matching how organic content works. First the brand video, then product tiles. It mirrors how friends share, not how billboards shout.

Dynamic product ad

Eye-tracking studies we ran showed that Snapchatters' attention goes to two places when watching an ad: the content itself, and the brand attribution. Almost no one looks at the bottom—where most CTAs traditionally lived. Visual attention concentrated near the top and center of the screen, while most of our legacy CTAs lived lower down. This insight led us to redesign where and how calls-to-action appear, bringing them closer to where people actually look.

A direct outcome of this insight is my redesign of DPA Collection Ads, a format to dynamically display advertisers' products from a catalog. I upgraded the tiny product tiles placed at the bottom to larger, more prominent product tiles placed in attention-dense areas so interaction aligned with where people naturally look.

This became the biggest product ad format evolution in the last 5 years.

Launching Sponsored Snaps: Snap's first ad format on Chat.

In 2024, we launched Sponsored Snaps—the first time Snapchat monetized the Chat tab, where people connect with their closest friends. It was the most sensitive surface we'd ever touched with advertising, and I led the 0→1 design of this format, defining the user experience, interaction model, and guardrails from scratch.

The design challenge

The Chat tab is where you talk to your best friends. Putting an ad there felt risky—even to us. The bar for 'feeling right' was higher than anything we'd done before.

Designed to feel like a Snap

A Sponsored Snap arrives just like any other Snap—sitting in your Chat feed, from a brand instead of a friend. You tap to open it. It feels immediate and personal, the way Snaps should. If the brand has a Story, it appears in the Chat header naturally. Everything was designed to respect the patterns people already know.

Real conversation, not just broadcast

Unlike traditional ads, Sponsored Snaps open into a Chat experience. Users can see links, saved Chats, even respond with freeform messages or prompts. The advertiser receives these responses. It's advertising that invites dialogue rather than demanding attention.

Impact

I filed 3 design patterns for this innovative new format. By bringing a native, chat-first ad experience to life, Sponsored Snaps outperformed benchmark formats in CTR and downstream actions. The format quickly became one of the most effective ways for advertisers to reach Snapchatters, supporting Snap's ads revenue growth in 2025.

Exploring New Monetization Opportunities

Beyond shipped features, a big part of my role was exploring what's next—concepts that push the business forward while staying true to Snapchat's experience. Many of these I pitched directly to leadership.

Sponsored Slots on Maps

We used to show ads mainly for physical stores, which captured only a small slice of the real economy. I proposed adding lightweight, contextual ad slots in places where people already expect to see promotion in the real world: for example, billboards along highways in Maps. That concept became Sponsored Slots on Maps. The pitch was well received and is being prioritized.

Creators’ birthday wishes

We've expanded Sponsored Snaps beyond brand takeovers. Creators can now send Snaps directly to their followers on special occasions like birthdays, creating direct, personal engagement with fans.

Making product ads feel personal

Dynamic Product Ads (DPA) often felt cold and catalog-like. I explored using audience signals—age, location, interests—to generate contextually relevant backgrounds with GenAI. A backpack shown in a college dorm for a student. The same backpack in an office for a young professional. Same product, but it suddenly feels like it belongs in your life.

Takeaway

Working in this space made it clear how sensitive monetization surfaces really are. Small decisions about timing, placement, and interaction can determine whether an ad earns attention or gets dismissed as noise.

The strongest results came not from pushing harder on CTAs, but from designing formats that respect user intent and give people time to decide. That principle continues to guide how I approach monetization design.
