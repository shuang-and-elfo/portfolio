Google Ads Smart Campaigns

During my time at Google Ads, I worked on AdWords Express, which later became Google Ads Smart Campaigns, a team focused on helping small businesses run ads on Google effectively. I completely redesigned and revamped the campaign creation and management experience; both launched with strong results. I also led the end to end design of automated business websites so small advertisers could run ads without having a site of their own.

Redesigning the Campaign Creation Experience

Our signup flow had 13 steps and a 14.5% conversion rate. It was long and repetitive. The visual design was outdated, the language was confusing, and it lacked proper guidance. We needed a complete overhaul.

The Process

From the drawing board to UX approval, we spent 4 months with 30+ iterations, testing and refining every step of the experience. We ran several rounds of testing and iterated the experience based on small advertisers‚Äô feedback.

What We Changed

The old flow had an outdated look, long and confusing copy, and an ad creation step that felt intimidating with irrelevant sample ads and complex budget choices. We replaced it with a clean, consistent design, clearer and more conversational language, auto generated ad text suggestions, smart defaults based on the advertiser‚Äôs vertical, and budget packages with clear price anchors.

Ad Creation: Before & After

The old ad creation step had a 24% drop-off rate. Advertisers felt intimidated and didn't know what to write. The sample ad was irrelevant to their vertical, making it hard to relate.

We fixed this with auto generated ad text suggestions, clear expectations about where ads would show, and more conversational language throughout.

Budget Selection: Before & After

15% of users dropped off at budget selection. Many tried to "test it out" with the lowest budget possible, which made their campaigns hard to succeed. Showing both views and clicks was confusing since we charge by clicks.

We introduced budget packages that offer stronger price anchors, and simplified to show only click estimates so users understand exactly what they're paying for.

Smart Defaults That Save Time

  • Business vertical ‚Üí Prefilled keywords: We recognized your industry and suggested relevant keywords
  • Business vertical ‚Üí Ad text suggestions: Auto-generated copy based on what works in your category
  • Sign-in account ‚Üí Business profile: Pulled your business info automatically
  • Business profile ‚Üí Website: Pre-populated from your existing web presence

After we fully launched in June 2020, the experience was so well received that it became the default campaign creation flow for all new Google advertisers, even though it was designed for small and medium businesses.

Redesigning Campaign Management

Small advertisers didn't know what actions to take looking at their dashboard. The old dashboard was full of empty states and offered no clear action items, so our retention rate was low. They'd set up a campaign and then... never come back.

What We Changed

In the redesign, we surface insights, offer education, and for advertisers at different stages we populated a set of cards customized for each business.

Key Insight

Small advertisers care less about fancy charts. They want to understand if "the ad is working," they want transparency to trust the product, and they want to know what actions to take to improve performance.

I explored different frameworks for a recommendation feed: gallery style, vertical feed, and horizontal style. We tested with advertisers at different stages and populated a set of cards customized for each business.

Ultimately, we decided to provide in context recommendations at the right spots, showing relevant actions when and where users could actually take them.
