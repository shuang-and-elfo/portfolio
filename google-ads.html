Redesigning the Signup Experience

13 steps. 14.5% conversion rate.

Our signup flow was long and repetitive. The visual design was outdated, the language was confusing, and it lacked proper guidance. We needed a complete overhaul.

The Process

From the drawing board to UX approval, we spent 4 months with 30+ iterations‚Äîtesting and refining every step of the experience.

What We Changed

Before
  • Outdated, enterprise-y look
  • Lengthy, confusing descriptions
  • Intimidating ad creation
  • Irrelevant sample ads
  • Complex budget selection
After
  • Clean, modern, consistent design
  • Conversational, helpful language
  • Auto-generated ad text suggestions
  • Smart defaults based on vertical
  • Budget packages with price anchors

Smart Defaults That Save Time

  • Business vertical ‚Üí Prefilled keywords: We recognized your industry and suggested relevant keywords
  • Business vertical ‚Üí Ad text suggestions: Auto-generated copy based on what works in your category
  • Sign-in account ‚Üí Business profile: Pulled your business info automatically
  • Business profile ‚Üí Website: Pre-populated from your existing web presence

Ad Creation: Before & After

The old ad creation step had a 24% drop-off rate. Advertisers felt intimidated and didn't know what to write. The sample ad was irrelevant to their vertical, making it hard to relate.

We fixed this with auto-generated ad text suggestions, clear expectations about where ads would show, and more conversational language throughout.

Budget Selection: Before & After

15% of users dropped off at budget selection. Many tried to "test it out" with the lowest budget possible‚Äîwhich made their campaigns hard to succeed. Showing both views and clicks was confusing since we charge by clicks.

We introduced budget packages that offer stronger price anchors, and simplified to show only click estimates so users understand exactly what they're paying for.

Campaign Management Redesign

Small advertisers didn't know what actions to take looking at their dashboard. As a result, our retention rate was low. They'd set up a campaign and then... never come back.

Key Insight

Small advertisers care less about fancy charts. They want to understand if "the ad is working," they want transparency to trust the product, and they want to know what actions to take to improve performance.

The Approach

I explored different frameworks for a recommendation feed: gallery style, vertical feed, and horizontal style. We tested with advertisers at different stages and populated a set of cards customized for each business.

Ultimately, we decided to provide in-context recommendations at the right spots‚Äîshowing relevant actions when and where users could actually take them.

Ads Landing Pages

The Challenge

How might we help advertisers create ads on the web without having a website?

Many small businesses‚Äîespecially in emerging markets‚Äîwanted to advertise but didn't have a website to send traffic to. This was a significant barrier to entry.

The Solution

We created an auto-generated business listing page with responsive loading, dynamic ordering, conversion tracking, and all the technical capabilities of a proper landing page.

The key insight was integrating this directly into the ad signup flow, minimizing the information advertisers need to provide. If they could describe their business, we could build them a page.

Reflection

Key Learnings

Working on Smart Campaigns taught me that the best simplification isn't about removing features‚Äîit's about removing cognitive load. Small business owners don't have time to become advertising experts, but they do have goals they want to achieve. Our job was to bridge that gap.

  • Smart defaults are powerful: Pre-filling based on context dramatically reduces friction
  • Transparency builds trust: Users need to understand what's happening, even if it's automated
  • Meet users where they are: Not everyone has a website, and that shouldn't prevent them from growing their business
  • Iterate relentlessly: 30+ iterations over 4 months‚Äîreal improvement comes from continuous refinement
