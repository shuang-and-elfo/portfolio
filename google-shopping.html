A More Personalized Homepage

The existing Google Shopping homepage was underutilized. Users went directly to search, missing opportunities for discovery. We set out to create a destination worth visiting‚Äîa place where shoppers could browse, discover, and find things they didn't know they wanted.

Design Goal

Create a homepage with really good recommendations‚Äîones that feel personally relevant, not just algorithmically generated.

The Approach

We leveraged Google's deep understanding of user intent‚Äîsearch history, browsing patterns, and cross-platform signals‚Äîto surface products that matched each user's interests and needs.

  • Personalized product feeds: Recommendations based on browsing history, search queries, and purchase patterns
  • Category discovery: Curated collections that introduce users to new product categories
  • Deal surfacing: Prominent placement for sales and limited-time offers to drive return visits
  • Price tracking: Let users follow products and get notified when prices drop

Key Insight

Shopping isn't just about finding specific items‚Äîit's also about discovery. By making browsing enjoyable and serendipitous, we could capture a different user intent entirely: the joy of window shopping, digitized.

Helping Shoppers Buy with Confidence

Online shopping comes with inherent uncertainty. Is this the right size? Is this a good deal? Will I regret this purchase? We explored ways to reduce this anxiety and help shoppers feel confident in their decisions.

The Challenge

How might we reduce purchase anxiety and help shoppers make informed decisions‚Äîespecially for considered purchases like clothing, electronics, and home goods?

Explorations

We explored multiple approaches across different product categories, testing with real users to understand what information they needed most at the moment of decision.

  • Rich product information: Detailed specs, sizing guides, and comparison tools
  • Social proof: Reviews, ratings, and real customer photos
  • Price context: Historical pricing, price comparisons across retailers
  • Return policies: Clear information about returns and guarantees

The explorations spanned categories from women's down jackets to Nike Air Max sneakers‚Äîunderstanding that different products require different confidence-building signals.

Reflection

Key Learnings

Competing with Amazon wasn't about copying their features‚Äîit was about finding our unique strengths. Google's search intelligence and cross-platform presence gave us insights that no other shopping platform had. The key was translating those signals into genuinely helpful shopping experiences.

  • Personalization must feel personal: Generic "recommended for you" isn't enough‚Äîusers can tell when recommendations are truly relevant
  • Discovery is undervalued: Not every shopping session starts with a specific intent‚Äîcreating space for browsing unlocks new engagement
  • Confidence drives conversion: Reducing anxiety at the moment of purchase is just as important as getting users to that moment
  • Play to your strengths: Being 10 years behind doesn't mean you can't win‚Äîit means you need to compete differently
