Case Study

Google Shopping

Providing unique value to shoppers in a mega-competitive space—creating a personalized homepage experience that helps users discover products and buy with confidence.

Role

Interaction Designer

Company

Google

Timeline

May 2019 – Nov 2019

The Challenge

Google Shopping was competing in a mega-competitive space—we were 10 years behind Amazon. To win, we couldn't just match the competition. We needed to provide unique value that shoppers couldn't find anywhere else. How might we leverage Google's unique strengths—search intelligence, personalization, and cross-platform presence—to create a shopping experience that feels distinctly valuable? We focused on two areas: a personalized homepage with smart recommendations tailored to each shopper, and features that help shoppers make informed decisions and trust their purchases.

A More Personalized Homepage

The existing Google Shopping homepage was underutilized. Users went directly to search, missing opportunities for discovery. We set out to create a destination worth visiting—a place where shoppers could browse, discover, and find things they didn't know they wanted.

Design Goal

Create a homepage with really good recommendations—ones that feel personally relevant, not just algorithmically generated.

The Approach

We leveraged Google's deep understanding of user intent—search history, browsing patterns, and cross-platform signals—to surface products that matched each user's interests and needs.

  • Personalized product feeds: Recommendations based on browsing history, search queries, and purchase patterns
  • Category discovery: Curated collections that introduce users to new product categories
  • Deal surfacing: Prominent placement for sales and limited-time offers to drive return visits
  • Price tracking: Let users follow products and get notified when prices drop

Helping Shoppers Buy with Confidence

Online shopping comes with inherent uncertainty. Is this the right size? Is this a good deal? Will I regret this purchase? We explored ways to reduce this anxiety and help shoppers feel confident in their decisions.

The Challenge

How might we reduce purchase anxiety and help shoppers make informed decisions—especially for considered purchases like clothing, electronics, and home goods?

Explorations

We explored multiple approaches across different product categories, testing with real users to understand what information they needed most at the moment of decision: rich product information (specs, sizing guides, comparison tools), social proof (reviews, ratings, real customer photos), price context (historical pricing, cross-retailer comparisons), and clear return policies. The explorations spanned categories from women's down jackets to Nike Air Max sneakers—understanding that different products require different confidence-building signals.

Explorations for helping shoppers buy with confidence—price context, rich product info, and decision support

Reflection

Competing with Amazon wasn't about copying their features—it was about finding our unique strengths. Google's search intelligence and cross-platform presence gave us insights no other shopping platform had; the real work was turning those signals into experiences that felt genuinely helpful. Personalization only works when it feels personal. Discovery is undervalued: not every session starts with a specific intent, and creating space for browsing unlocks a different kind of engagement. Reducing anxiety at the moment of purchase is just as important as getting users there. Being 10 years behind doesn't mean you can't win—it means you have to compete differently.
